Dr. Theresa Tam

Dr. Theresa Tam (born in 1965 in Hong Kong) is a physician and public servant who currently serves as the 3rd chief public health officer of Canada.

As she’s the chief public health officer of Canada and head of the Public Health Agency of Canada, Canadians have grown accustomed to seeing Dr. Theresa Tam in nearly daily briefings during the coronavirus pandemic. Prior to managing Canada’s response to COVID-19, Tam helped guide Canada through health crises like SARS, H1N1 and Ebola. Her background as a pediatric infectious disease specialist makes her a highly knowledgeable and pragmatic leader. She is undoubtedly one of the most important figures in Canada’s public health history.

Accomplishment
She attended medical school at the University of Nottingham, earning a BMBS. She completed her pediatric residency at the University of Alberta, and a pediatric infectious diseases fellowship at University of British Columbia.[5][6]
Tam was a co-chair of a 2006 federal report that year on pandemic preparedness in the wake of the SARS outbreak in Canada
She was Director of the Immunization and Respiratory Infections Division at the PHAC in 2006
Tam has played a leadership role in Canada's response to public health emergencies including SARS, H1N1, Ebola and now COVID-19.
She is on the Independent Oversight and Advisory Committee of the Health Emergencies Programme of the World Health Organization.
She is the current Federal chief health officer of Canada
